Love Plane

Banksy

Originally adorning the side of Norwich House opposite the Liverpool War Museum, Love Plane is a 15-feet tall two-colour spray stencil of a biplane trailing a heart-shaped smoke trail — created using a fire extinguisher filled with white paint. A technically ambitious work showcasing Banksy's craft at its most precise, it was carefully salvaged and restored for permanent display at Baltic House, the world's first dedicated street art museum. Now offered privately for the first time, the work is accompanied by full provenance documentation and authentication from recognised experts.

  • Medium: Spray paint on concrete
  • Dimensions: 15 feet tall (approx. 457 cm)

Estimated Value: USD 3,000,000 – 9,000,000

White House Rat

Banksy, 2004

Created during the Liverpool Biennial in 2004, White House Rat is a monumental spray paint mural spanning 300 × 400 cm on the façade of the Whitehouse Pub — a Grade II-listed building in Liverpool's historic Chinatown. The work depicts a giant stencilled rat clutching a marker pen, drawing direct lineage from French street artist Blek le Rat, and channelling Banksy's signature themes of rebellion and socio-political critique. Salvaged in 2011, this rare work is accompanied by full provenance documentation and authentication from recognised experts.

  • Medium: Spray paint on concrete
  • Dimensions: 300 × 400 cm

Estimated Value: USD 3,000,000 – 9,000,000
